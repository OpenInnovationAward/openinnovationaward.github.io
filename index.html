State High Open Innovation Scholarship

Contribute!

Purpose

This scholarship will provide recognition and cash awards to graduating State High students who do extraordinary things in fulfillment of the senior project requirement. The award will be advertised during the 2015-2016 school year and awarded for the first time in May 2016. Winning projects will demonstrate substantial independent learning, innovative exploration of the DIY arts and sciences, and “open source” sharing to encourage others to create, collaborate, and extend.

Application

Applicants will submit a multimedia project portfolio, hosted on a free online platform, that shares and documents an innovative innovative open source project in the arts, science, or engineering—preferably all three! The portfolio must be "open source"—it must document the project in sufficient detail for someone else to recreate and extend their work. Applicants are encouraged to incorporate freely-available open source software and easily acquired open source hardware into their projects. Works that involve the design, construction, and/or use of do-it-yourself tools are especially encouraged to apply.

Almost anything could be a good candidate for this award:

  • DIY musical instruments, synths, speakers, amps, effects pedals
  • DIY scientific tools: amateur radio, meteorology, biology, chemistry, physics, astronomy, &etc
  • education outreach projects; 'getting started' and 'how-to' guides, sites, and wikis
  • explorations of unusual or innovative artistic media
  • scientific experiments and open-source engineering projects
  • open source computing, software, cryptography, simulations
  • practical creations: horticulture, repair/repurposing/recycling, community projects

For inspiration, check out makezine.com, makerspace.com, /r/DIY, and other cool places on the Internet.
