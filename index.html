State High Open Innovation Scholarship

Planning Document (Restricted)

Purpose

This scholarship aims to publicly recognize exceptional effort on the State High senior project. Winning projects will demonstrate substantial independent learning, innovative exploration of the DIY arts and sciences, and “open source” sharing with the wider Maker/DIY community.

Application

Graduating State High seniors may apply by submitting a web portfolio of original work, hosted in GitHub Pages, that shares and documents an innovative open source project in engineering, science, or the arts (or all three!). A project can be almost anything: creation and application of a new tool or a useful or interesting method or device; a guide to getting started in an unusual art or craft; a scientific experiment that answers interesting questions in an innovative way. While you may use specialized tools, components of the project should be reasonably easy to acquire or build, all software used should be free and open source, and the online documentation you create must be complete enough for someone else to recreate your work on their own. The more “from the ground, up” the better—bonus points for making the tools necessary to practice your art.

For inspiration, check out makezine.com, makerspace.com, /r/DIY, and other cool places on the Internet.

Award

The top three applicants will receive a share of an $(INSERT NUMBER) unrestricted cash award to continue their education and independent exploration. Additional exceptional entries may be recognized.

Questions? Email open.innovation.award@gmail.com

For more information and previous years’ entries, see http://openinnovationaward.github.io
